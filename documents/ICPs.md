# Drafta AI — Ideal Customer Profiles

> Dossiers for each customer archetype. Use these to guide feature prioritization, copy, onboarding, and UX decisions.

---

## ICP 1: Solo Founder / Indie Hacker

**Who**: Technical or semi-technical founders building products solo or with a tiny team (1-3 people).

**Demographics**: Age 25-40, startup ecosystem, remote-first, uses multiple SaaS tools daily.

**What they need**:
- Fast way to organize thoughts, plans, and data in one place
- AI that understands their product context and can help draft docs, analyze data, create decks
- Quick pitch deck generation for investors or partners
- Spreadsheet analysis without Excel expertise
- Document creation that doesn't require switching to Google Docs

**What they want to do**:
- "I just want to type what I need and have AI figure out the format"
- Create investor updates, pitch decks, financial models in minutes
- Keep everything in one workspace instead of 5 different tools

**Pain points**:
- Context-switching between Notion, Google Sheets, Google Slides, ChatGPT
- Re-explaining context to AI every time
- Tools that are either too simple (ChatGPT) or too complex (full office suites)

**Willingness to pay**: $15-30/month — values time savings over cost

---

## ICP 2: Content Creator / Marketer

**Who**: Marketing professionals, content strategists, freelance writers who create varied content types.

**Demographics**: Age 22-45, agency or in-house, manages multiple projects/clients.

**What they need**:
- AI writing assistant that maintains brand voice across documents
- Data visualization from campaign metrics (spreadsheets → diagrams)
- Presentation decks for client pitches and reports
- Quick turnaround on content drafts

**What they want to do**:
- Turn raw data into visual stories
- Generate first drafts of blog posts, reports, proposals
- Create client-facing decks without a designer

**Pain points**:
- Spending hours formatting instead of creating
- Inconsistent outputs from generic AI tools
- Can't easily go from data → insight → presentation

**Willingness to pay**: $20-50/month (or per-seat for teams)

---

## ICP 3: Small Business Operator

**Who**: Non-technical business owners who need to create documents, track data, and present information.

**Demographics**: Age 30-55, runs a business with 5-50 employees, limited tech budget.

**What they need**:
- Simple, guided interface — no learning curve
- AI that can create professional documents from plain English
- Basic spreadsheet functionality for budgets, inventories, tracking
- Presentation decks for team meetings, board updates

**What they want to do**:
- "Make me a budget spreadsheet for Q2"
- "Create a slide deck for our quarterly review"
- "Write a proposal for [client name]"

**Pain points**:
- Intimidated by complex tools (Excel, PowerPoint)
- Can't afford a dedicated designer or writer
- Needs professional-looking output without professional skills

**Willingness to pay**: $10-25/month — ROI-sensitive, needs clear value

---

## ICP 4: Student / Researcher

**Who**: University students, graduate researchers, academic professionals.

**Demographics**: Age 18-35, budget-conscious, high volume of document creation.

**What they need**:
- Research organization (notes, references, data)
- AI help with structuring papers, analyzing datasets
- Diagram creation for thesis presentations
- Quick presentation decks for classes/conferences

**What they want to do**:
- Organize research notes and turn them into structured papers
- Analyze survey/experiment data without learning R or Python
- Create presentation slides from their research

**Pain points**:
- Information scattered across tools
- Formatting takes longer than writing
- Limited budget for premium tools

**Willingness to pay**: $5-15/month (freemium-sensitive, upgrade for AI features)

---

## Cross-Cutting Insights

| Signal | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| All ICPs hate context-switching | Single workspace is the core value prop |
| Non-technical users are 2 of 4 ICPs | UI must be simple-first, power features progressive |
| "Just tell AI what I need" is universal | Chat-first UX is correct architecture |
| Decks + docs are highest frequency | Prioritize these over sheets/diagrams |
| Price sensitivity varies 3x | Freemium + tiered pricing essential |
