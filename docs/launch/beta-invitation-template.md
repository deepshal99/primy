# Beta Invitation Playbook

Outreach kit for the first 50 hand-picked beta users. Goal: get them in the product, get testimonials and screenshots, build word-of-mouth before the public launch.

---

## Selection criteria

We want users who:
1. Will actually use the product (not collectors of beta access)
2. Are loud — they post about tools they like
3. Fit one of the project-based work archetypes (founder / consultant / marketer / creator)
4. Are reachable via Twitter or LinkedIn DM

### Concrete archetypes (5)

For each, examples of who to look for + where:

#### A. Indie founders / build-in-public crowd (15-20 of the 50)
- Active on Twitter/X with 1k-50k followers
- Tweet about their own products / engineering / product decisions
- Search: `"shipping" + "side project"`, `"build in public"` filter to people you've replied to
- Communities: IndieHackers active members, Levels.io's reply guys, Tony Dinh's followers, the Lenny Substack comment crowd

#### B. Solo consultants / fractional execs (10-15)
- LinkedIn job titles: "Fractional CMO," "Independent consultant," "Strategy advisor," "Solo consultant"
- 5-15 years experience, run their own engagements (not at McKinsey/BCG)
- Active in: LinkedIn comments under marketing/ops content, Polywork members, Pavilion members

#### C. Newsletter operators / creators (5-10)
- Substack/beehiiv writers with 1k-10k subs
- Write about: business, productivity, tools, marketing, creator economy
- Search: people who quote-tweet Lenny Rachitsky / Justin Welsh

#### D. Solo agency owners (5-10)
- Twitter or LinkedIn: "founder of [small agency]"
- Specifically: branding, marketing, content, design agencies under 5 people
- Reachable via reply guys in agency niche

#### E. Heavy ChatGPT users vocal about workflow (5-10)
- Tweet things like "I use ChatGPT for [X]"
- Have a specific recurring workflow they describe
- Easy "have you tried" hook

## Outreach DM templates

### Twitter / X DM (140 chars max if possible)

> Hey [name] — I built Primy to kill the ChatGPT copy-paste loop for project work. Want a Pro pass (60 days, free)? Curious what you'd build with it.

If they reply with interest:

> Sweet. Quick context: it's an AI workspace that builds docs, sheets, and decks in one place — keeps memory across files. Sign up at primy.so/login?mode=signup with this email so I can flip on Pro: [their email]
>
> All I ask: tell me what works and what's broken after a week of using it. Even just 3 lines is gold.

### LinkedIn DM (longer, more formal)

> Hi [name] — really enjoyed [their recent post / podcast / article].
>
> I'm building Primy — an AI workspace for project work. Drag in your files, chat to create docs, sheets, and decks, with project memory across everything. Solo founder, India-based, just shipped v1.0.
>
> I'm hand-picking 50 beta users for the first cohort. Pro tier free for 60 days. The only ask: feedback after a week. Honest, no spin.
>
> If you're curious, here's the link: primy.so. Sign up with your work email and I'll flip on Pro for you.
>
> No worries if not your thing — happy to support your work however I can.
>
> — Deepak

## Follow-up if no response (one nudge, max)

Send 5 days after initial DM. NEVER more than once.

> Hey [name] — circling back once. If the timing's off, no worries. If you'd like that Pro pass, just reply "in" and I'll send the signup link. Either way, all good.

## Onboarding email (sent after they sign up)

Auto-send when a user with a beta-list email signs up. Manual for v1.0.

Subject: `welcome to primy — quick orientation`

> Hi [name],
>
> Welcome — and thanks for jumping in early. You're on Pro for 60 days, no payment needed (we don't even have payments live yet).
>
> Three things that'll help you in the first 10 minutes:
>
> 1. **Drop a file you're already working with.** A real client brief, last quarter's deck, a CSV of your numbers. The AI reads them and uses them as context for everything you ask next. This is the "wait, that's actually useful" moment.
>
> 2. **Try a slash command.** `/proposal` for proposals, `/status` for status updates, `/qbr` for QBR decks. Type the slash, hit enter, watch what happens.
>
> 3. **Don't try to learn the whole product.** Pick one thing on your plate this week and try to do it in Primy. Tell me where you got stuck.
>
> When you have feedback (good, bad, ugly), reply to this email. I read every one.
>
> If you want to skip the onboarding flow and jump straight in: primy.so/app
>
> — Deepak
> P.S. The product is actively shipping. Expect 2-3 visible improvements per week.

## Weekly feedback ask (every Friday for 4 weeks)

Subject: `quick: what's working, what's not?`

> Hi [name],
>
> One-line ask: what's working, what's not?
>
> If you have 30 seconds, even bullet-point answers help me prioritize next week.
>
> If you used Primy this week and have a screenshot to share, I'd love to feature you (with permission) on Twitter. Tag primy.so or just reply.
>
> — Deepak

After 4 weeks if no engagement: stop emailing. Save the relationship, don't pester.

---

## Testimonial request (week 3 if engaged)

Send only if they've used the product 3+ times in the last week.

Subject: `would you mind a 2-min favor?`

> Hi [name],
>
> Bit of a favor: I'm collecting honest testimonials for the launch.
>
> If you'd write 2-3 sentences about how Primy has fit into your workflow (the good and the limits), I'd love to use it on the site, with your name + role + permission to link to your profile.
>
> If now's a bad time, no worries — happy to ask again later.
>
> Suggested prompts (use any or none):
> - What was the last project where Primy saved you real time?
> - What did you stop doing because of Primy?
> - What's still missing?
>
> — Deepak

## Tracking

Keep a simple spreadsheet (or use Primy itself once it's stable!):

| Name | Channel (TW/LI/Email) | DM sent | Replied | Signed up | Activated (>=1 artifact) | Week 1 feedback | Testimonial? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Goal:
- 80% reply rate (the cold-but-warm strategy works if you've actually engaged with their content first)
- 60% signup rate from replies
- 40% activation rate
- 20% testimonials

That gives 50 beta DMs → 40 replies → 24 signups → 10 active → 5 testimonials. Adjust outreach volume to hit your targets.
